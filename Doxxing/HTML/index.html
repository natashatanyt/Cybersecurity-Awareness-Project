Introduction

Doxxing occurs when personally identifiable information is posted online, with the intent of causing harm/distress. This may happen as an act of revenge, putting the victim in an unsafe and stressful situation.

People may share a person’s information, believing it is justified under the guise of social justice. Victims of doxxing may be stalked, or face threats of violence. In Singapore, doxxing is illegal and attackers will face consequences.

How Information is Gathered

Tracking Usernames

People use the same username on different platforms and accounts, making it easy for followers to find them on multiple websites. However, it is also easy for an attacker to find accounts and collate shared information.

Phishing

Phishing is an easy way for attackers to gain access to a person’s sensitive information. With access to their account, attackers can view information that the person chose to hide from the public, making it easy for them to gather information about their victim.

Stalking on Social Media

If your account is public, anyone can look through your posts. If you are careless with what you share, attackers can figure out your personal information from looking at your posts, such as your address or favorite places.
