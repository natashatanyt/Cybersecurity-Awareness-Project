Staying Safe Online

This website is for parents, meant to educate them on various topics which can affect their children. Each page covers specific topics, explaining what it is and its importance, along with additional resources and references.

At the bottom of this page, there are games you can play with your child. These games aim to educate your child about various concepts.

Topics

Clickbait & Misinformation

With news being spread easily online, it is easy for less reputable sites to post misleading information. It is important to be able to identify fake news so you are not misled.

Learn about it here!

Doxxing

People are prone to oversharing online, especially when they believe they are among friends. When disagreements arise, some may doxx as a form of revenge.

Learn about it here!

Malware

Malware can infect devices unknowingly, resulting in a loss of data and privacy. Attackers can spy on their victims, tracking them across websites and stealing login information.

Learn about it here!

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA improves the security of an account, making it more difficult for attackers to gain access to your details, even if they have access to your username and password.

Learn about it here!

Gaming

Online gaming allows children to spend time with their friends while in different physical locations. However, there are problems that children may face while playing these games.

Learn about it here!

Password Safety

Having a strong password is the foundation of keeping your accounts safe. A weak password makes you prone to falling for various attacks. Luckily, a strong password is easy to make.

Learn about it here!

Social Engineering

Phishing and other forms of social engineering attacks are becoming more complex. Additionally, attackers are more discreet in terms of how they gather information about you.

Learn about it here!

Social Media

People use social media to keep in contact with friends and share about their lives. However, your child may be a target of harmful interactions, where others seek to exploit them.

Learn about it here!

Additional Games

Think you know it all? Test out your knowledge with these games! These are all from external online sources, all collated for easy reference and access on this website.

Texas A&M University

The following games were created by Texas A&M University, Division of Information Technology. It covers a range of topics, from general cyber practices to identifying phishing emails.

  • Football Fever
  • Fight Back
  • Missing Link

Spoofy

Spoofy is a game created for Finnish primary school students to teach them about cybersecurity. For more information, a downloadable PDF is available on their website.

Reality Check

Reality Check is a game made by MediaSmarts, Canada's Center for Media and Digital Literacy. Along with many other resources, this game helps your child better identify fake news and clickbait.

Interland

Interland is part of an internet safety program for kids, aged 8 to 12, created by Google, with four different worlds, each emphasizing a different topic, such as phishing or managing cyberbullying.

Other References

Cybersecurity Agency (CSA) has launched a campaign, SG Cyber Safe, with resources for students in the form of a comic book series. Additionally, CSA has a series of videos which can be found here.

Kaspersky also has a few educational courses to teach you about various dangers and how to stay safe online, which can be found here.
