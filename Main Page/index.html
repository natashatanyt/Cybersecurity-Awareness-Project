Staying Safe Online

This website is meant for parents, either to browse on their own or together with their children. Each page goes deeper in-depth, explaining what each topic is, and why it is important, and includes additional resources and references. Each page also has a short quiz.

There are additional external game websites that you can visit, which seek to educate your child and reinforce previously explained concepts. If you are unfamiliar with these topics, it is recommended that you play these games with your child, as the games teach concepts in an engaging and understandable way.

Topics

Clickbait & Misinformation

These days, most people get their news online. However, for various reasons, less reputable news sites post misleading or wrong information. It is important to be able to differentiate between real and fake news so that you are not misled.

Learn about it here!

Doxxing

Most people are fairly open with the information they post online, especially when they believe they are among only friends. When disagreements arise, some attackers may choose to doxx as a form of revenge. By learning about what doxxing is, you will be able to better prevent others from being able to doxx you.

Learn about it here!

Malware

Malware is can be found everywhere, and can easily infect devices of trusting and gullible users. It can also result in a loss of data as attackers can also spy on their victims, tracking them across websites and stealing their login information.

Learn about it here!

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA improves and increases the security of an account. If attackers do manage to obtain your login details, they will need to pass another layer of authentication to gain access to your account.

Learn about it here!

Gaming

Many children these days play online games, allowing them to keep in contact with real-life friends and to meet new online friends. While most interactions will not bring any harm to your child, they should be aware and equipped to handle any trouble that comes their way.

Learn about it here!

Password Safety

The most important and basic form of cybersecurity safety is a strong password. Even with all the knowledge and other good cybersecurity practices, using a weak password will still make you prone to falling for attacks. Luckily, making a strong password is not difficult to do.

Learn about it here!

Social Engineering

Many people know of phishing emails, and believe that they are able to spot the signs. However, these attacks are increasingly harder to spot, and the way attackers gather information about you is becoming more discreet. Learn about the different types of attacks and additional steps you can take to keep your accounts safe.

Learn about it here!

Social Media

Most people have social media as a way to share about their lives and to keep in contact with friends. However, there are many dangers when it comes to online interactions. By teaching your child to be safe online, you will prevent them from being affected by those with malicious intent, even without your supervision.

Learn about it here!

Additional Games

Think you know it all? Test out your knowledge with these games! These are all from external online sources, all collated for easy reference and access on this website.

The following games were created by Texas A&M University, Division of Information Technology. It covers a range of topics, from general cyber practices to identifying phishing emails.

  • Football Fever
  • Fight Back
  • Missing Link

Spoofy is a game created for Finnish primary school students to teach them about cybersecurity. For more information, a downloadable PDF is available on their website.

  • Spoofy

MediaSmarts is Canada's Center for Media and Digital Literacy. Along with many other resources, this game helps your child better identify fake news and clickbait.

  • Reality Check

Interland is part of an internet safety program for kids, aged 8 to 12, created by Google, with four different worlds, each emphasizing a different topic, such as phishing or managing cyberbullying.

  • Interland
