Which of the following statements are true about OTPs?

A

It can be received with a physical or digital token

B

It is generated periodically

C

It can be made of alphanumeric characters

D

All of the above

OTPs are meant to be difficult for attackers to gain access to. Hence, it can be sent through SMS, email, or applications. Each time it is needed, a new code is generated, preventing attackers from being able to log in with old codes. Some codes are made of a combination of letters and numbers, which makes it more difficult for attackers to guess.

Which of the following is not an example of MFA?

A

Entering your password twice

B

A password and SMS OTP

C

An access key card and passcode

D

All of the above

Entering your password twice is a type of verification method, as it verifies that the user entered the correct information. The other two examples involve two different methods of verification, including both physical and virtual methods.

Other than MFA, how can you keep your account safe?

A

A strong password

B

Do not use the same password everywhere

C

Keep your accounts to yourself

D

All of the above

Multi-factor authentication is a good way to keep your account safe, but cannot be solely relied on to keep your accounts safe. To ensure your account stays secure, you should use a unique, strong password, that has not been used for other accounts. Additionally, do not share your account with others as you do not know if they will share it or misuse it.

Why is MFA important?

A

It is not important

B

It increases the security of your account

C

It increases the time needed to log in

D

None of the above

MFA is important as it increases the security of your account. It prevents other people from logging in as they need access to verification methods that only you have access to. Even if attackers were to obtain your username and password, they would not be able to access your account.

If attackers obtain your password, what can they do?

A

Log in to your accounts

B

Pretend to be you online

C

Find out my personal information

D

All of the above

With access to your accounts, the attackers can impersonate you, act with your credentials, and attempt to trick your contacts into falling for phishing attempts. Additionally, if there is information on those accounts you want to keep hidden from others, attackers will have access to it and could potentially post it publicly online for others to see or use to blackmail you.
