When should you share your password with friends?

A

You should never share your password

B

If they ask for it

C

If they are helping you do something on that account

D

None of the above

You should never share your password with your friends. They may share your account with others without your knowledge, who may misuse your account. Additionally, if you do not change your password before sharing the account with them, they could log in to your other accounts using the same username/email and password. This would allow them to impersonate you online.

Which of the following are traits for a strong password?

A

Use the same password as other accounts

B

Use personal information

C

Keep it short so it is easy to remember

D

None of the above

A strong password should not contain personal information, be used repeatedly, or be too short. For a password to be strong, it should be at least 12 characters, containing a mix of upper and lower case letters, numbers, and symbols. A good way to make a strong password is to use a passphrase or diceware.

What should I do if my account has been compromised?

A

Reset my password

B

Enable multi-factor authentication

C

Check if other accounts have been compromised

D

All of the above

If your account has been compromised, you should check whether your other accounts have also been compromised. It is important to reset your password on all of the accounts which use that password, as attackers can easily find all your accounts that use the same email and password. Lastly, to increase security, enable multi-factor authentication, if possible, to reduce the chances of attackers gaining access to your accounts in the future.
