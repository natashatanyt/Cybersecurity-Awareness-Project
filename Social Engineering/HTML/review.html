What is the most common method of social engineering?

A

Phishing

B

Baiting

C

Scareware

D

Equally common

Phishing attacks are becoming increasingly common, especially since these attacks can be carried out and sent to a large number of people at once. Moreover, as it relies on the weak security practices of the victims and manipulates them, it is easier for attackers to successfully trick others.

You are walking down a very busy road when you see a thumb drive on the ground. It is in a very obvious place, and you dont see anyone going towards it. What should you do?

A

Leave it there

B

Bring it home

C

Put it somewhere less obvious

D

None of the above

If you see a thumbdrive on the ground, you should leave it there. It may be a baiting attack, which downloads malware into your device when inserted. If it does belong to someone who misplaced it, they will likely retrace their steps to try and find their thumbdrive. By leaving it there, is it easier for them to spot the thumbdrive.

You are about to play an online game with your friend, when you get a notification from the website that viruses have been detected on your device. What type of attack is this?

A

Phishing

B

Scareware

C

Baiting

D

None of the above

Scareware manipulates you by scaring you into believing your device is infected or faulty in some way. It tries to trick you into downloading malicious software under the guise that it will solve the issue you are currently facing. If you worry that your device is infected, run a scan on your device.

What information can you share online

A

Phone number

B

Address

C

Full name

D

Favourite food

You should never share information that can be used to figure out who you are, as it will allow strangers to stalk you, or get close to you by pretending to be someone close to your family. You can still share some information, such as your favorite food, color, or music genre. Anything that will help strangers figure out where you live, what school you are from or places you visit frequently should be kept secret.

Which of the following are signs that you have received a phishing email?

A

URL in the message is different from the URL when your mouse cursor hovers over the link

B

The name of the organization is misspelled

C

The sender's email address is different from the organization they claim to be from

D

All of the above

Once you learn the signs, phishing emails are relatively easy to spot. The signs include having a URL that directs to a different link, a misspelled organization name, or if the sender has a different email than the organization they claim to be from. Other signs include spelling mistakes in the body of the email, or if the email urges you to act immediately.
